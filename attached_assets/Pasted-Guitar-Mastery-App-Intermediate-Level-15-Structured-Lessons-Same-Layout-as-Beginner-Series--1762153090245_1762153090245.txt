Guitar Mastery App – Intermediate Level
(15 Structured Lessons – Same Layout as Beginner Series)

Lesson 1: Intermediate Barre Chords – Full Shapes & Root Notes
Objective: Master movable barre chords using E- and A-based shapes across the fretboard.
Key Concepts:
- E-based barre (roots on 6th string): F, F♯, G, etc.
- A-based barre (roots on 5th string): B♭, B, C, etc.
- Proper thumb position & wrist alignment to reduce fatigue.

Practice Exercises:
1. F Barre Chord Drill – Strum F (1st fret) 4x down, lift, repeat 10x.
2. Chromatic Barre Shift – F → F♯ → G → G♯ (E-shape) at 1-2-3-4 frets, 1 strum each.
3. A-Based Cycle – B♭ → B → C → C♯ (5th-string root) at 1-2-3-4 frets.

Song Application: “Wonderwall” (Oasis) – Em7 → G → Dsus4 → A7sus4 with partial barre transitions.

Homework: Record clean F and B♭ barre chords (no buzz) at 60 BPM.

Lesson 2: Power Chords & Palm Muting
Objective: Build rock rhythm foundation with 5th chords and controlled muting.
Key Concepts:
- Two-note power chords (root + 5th, optional octave).
- Palm mute technique: rest edge of picking hand on bridge.
- Dynamic control: muted vs. open strums.

Practice Exercises:
1. Root 6 Power Chord Shapes – E5 (022xxx), F5 (133xxx), G5 (355xxx) – 4x downstroke each.
2. Palm Mute Riff –
E|------------------------|
B|------------------------|
G|------------------------|
D|------0-0-3-3-5-5-3-3---|
A|------0-0-3-3-5-5-3-3---|
E|------0-0-3-3-5-5-3-3---|
   PM...................
   Play at 80 BPM, 8 reps.
3. Accent Drill – Alternate muted 8th notes with accented open chords.

Song Application: “Sweet Child O’ Mine” intro riff (D5-C5-G5).

Homework: 2-minute loop of palm-muted E5 riff at 100 BPM.

Lesson 3: Pentatonic Scale Position 2 & Connecting Shapes
Objective: Expand lead vocabulary with A minor pentatonic Position 2 and shape linking.
Key Concepts:
- Position 2: root on 5th string (A at 12th fret).
- Two-notes-per-string patterns for fluid phrasing.
- Connecting Position 1 → 2 using slides.

Practice Exercises:
1. Position 2 Ascending/Descending –
E|----------------------12-15-|
B|-----------------13-15------|
G|-------------12-14----------|
D|--------12-14---------------|
A|---12-15--------------------|
E|-15-------------------------|
   4x at 60 BPM triplets.
2. Shape Link Drill – Position 1 (5th fret) → slide to Position 2 (8th fret).
3. Call & Response – Play 4-note phrase in Pos 1, answer in Pos 2.

Song Application: Improv over “Back in Black” jam track (E minor).

Homework: Memorize Position 2 pattern; record 30-sec solo linking both positions.

Lesson 4: Intermediate Fingerstyle – Travis Picking Basics
Objective: Introduce thumb-independence pattern for folk/acoustic styles.
Key Concepts:
- Travis picking: alternating bass (thumb) + syncopated melody (fingers).
- Standard pattern: Thumb (6-5-4-5) + pinch i/m on beats 2 & 4.

Practice Exercises:
1. Bass Line Drill – Thumb only:
E|----------0-----------0----|
B|-------1-----------1-------|
G|-----2-----------2---------|
D|---2-----------2-----------|
A|-0-----------0-------------|
E|---------------------------|
   Repeat in G, C, D.
2. Pinch Pattern – Add index on G string, middle on B string:
Beat: 1  +  2  +  3  +  4  +
Thumb: 6  5  –  5  4  –  5  –
Fingers: –  –  i  –  –  m  –  –
3. Full Pattern in C – 4 measures, 80 BPM.

Song Application: “Dust in the Wind” intro (simplified Travis).

Homework: Play 1-minute Travis pattern in G → Em → C → D.

Lesson 5: 7th Chords & Dominant Function
Objective: Add color with maj7, min7, dom7 chords; understand V7→I resolution.
Key Concepts:
- Chord formulas:
  - Maj7: 1-3-5-7
  - Min7: 1-♭3-5-♭7
  - Dom7: 1-3-5-♭7
- Voice leading: common tones & smooth transitions.

Practice Exercises:
1. Open 7th Voicings –
   - G7 (320001)
   - Cm7 (x35343)
   - Fmaj7 (xx3210)
2. ii-V-I in G – Am7 → D7 → Gmaj7 (4 beats each, 70 BPM).
3. Turnaround – | Gmaj7 | Em7 | Am7 | D7 |

Song Application: “Autumn Leaves” – Cm7 → F7 → B♭maj7 → E♭maj7.

Homework: Transpose ii-V-I to keys of C and D; record clean changes.

Lesson 6: Hammer-Ons, Pull-Offs & Trills
Objective: Develop legato technique for fluid lead lines.
Key Concepts:
- Hammer-on: strike string with fretting finger.
- Pull-off: pluck string while lifting finger.
- Trill: rapid hammer/pull between two notes.

Practice Exercises:
1. Single-String Legato –
E|----------------5h8p5---------------|
B|------------5h8-------8p5-----------|
G|--------5h7---------------7p5-------|
D|----5h7-----------------------7p5---|
A|5h7-------------------------------7-|
   4x at 80 BPM.
2. Pentatonic Trill Drill – Trill between notes 1 beat each (12th & 15th fret).
3. Cross-String Legato –
E|--------12h15p12---------|
B|----13------------13-----|
G|-14------------------14--|

Song Application: “Stairway to Heaven” solo phrase (legato triplets).

Homework: 1-minute legato phrase over Am backing track.

Lesson 7: Intermediate Blues Rhythm – Shuffle & 9th Chords
Objective: Master shuffle feel and dominant 9th voicings.
Key Concepts:
- Shuffle: triplet feel (long-short-long-short).
- Dom9 chord: 1-3-5-♭7-9 (e.g., E9: 07677x).

Practice Exercises:
1. Shuffle Strum –
| 1 + a 2 + a 3 + a 4 + a |
| d   u   d   u   d   u   |
   Accent beats 2 & 4.
2. 12-Bar Blues in A with 9ths –
   | A9 | D9 | A9 | A9 |
   | D9 | D9 | A9 | A9 |
   | E9 | D9 | A9 | E9 |
3. Turnaround Lick –
E|-------------------------|
B|-------------------------|
G|-------------------------|
D|-------5-7-5-------------|
A|-5-6-7-------7-5---------|
E|-----------------7-5-----|

Song Application: “The Thrill Is Gone” (B.B. King).

Homework: Record 12-bar blues with shuffle and 9th chords at 90 BPM.

Lesson 8: Modes Intro – Dorian & Mixolydian
Objective: Understand and apply Dorian (minor) and Mixolydian (dominant) modes.
Key Concepts:
- Dorian: 1-2-♭3-4-5-6-♭7 (e.g., D Dorian over Cmaj7).
- Mixolydian: 1-2-3-4-5-6-♭7 (e.g., G Mixo over G7).

Practice Exercises:
1. D Dorian Scale (root on 10th fret, A string):
E|---------------------10-12-|
B|---------------10-12-------|
G|----------9-10-------------|
D|----9-10-12----------------|
A|-12------------------------|
2. Mode Phrase – Over Am7 → D7:
   - Am7: play D Dorian phrase.
   - D7: switch to D Mixolydian (same notes, different root).
3. Drone Jam – Improvise over static Am7 chord using Dorian.

Song Application: “Oye Como Va” (Santana) – Am7-D7 (Dorian/Mixolydian).

Homework: Write 8-bar solo alternating Dorian/Mixolydian.

Lesson 9: Alternate Picking Accuracy – Speed Bursts
Objective: Increase picking precision and speed with economy of motion.
Key Concepts:
- Strict alternate picking (down-up-down-up).
- Inside/outside picking for string changes.
- Metronome bursts: 4 notes at target speed, rest, repeat.

Practice Exercises:
1. Single String Burst –
E|--------------------------------|
B|--------------------------------|
G|-9-10-9-10-9-10-9-10-9-10-9-10--|
D|--------------------------------|
   4-note bursts at 120 → 140 → 160 BPM.
2. String Crossing –
E|-------12-------|-------10-------|
B|----13-------13-|----12-------12-|
G|-14-------14----|-12-------12----|
   Down-up strict.
3. Spider Exercise – 3-5-7-9 across strings.

Song Application: “Master of Puppets” gallop riff (clean alternate picking).

Homework: 2-minute alternate picking loop at 140 BPM.

Lesson 10: Intermediate Strumming – 16th Note Patterns
Objective: Master syncopated 16th-note strums with ghost notes.
Key Concepts:
- 16th grid: 1 e + a 2 e + a etc.
- Ghost strum: light upstroke, barely touching strings.

Practice Exercises:
1. Pattern 1 –
1 e + a 2 e + a 3 e + a 4 e + a
D     D   U   D U   U   D U
   In Em at 90 BPM.
2. Pattern 2 (Reggae-Inspired) –
1 e + a 2 e + a
  U   U     U   U
   Upstrokes only on off-beats.
3. Dynamic Layering – Add palm mute on downbeats.

Song Application: “Santeria” (Sublime) – 16th-note upbeat strums.

Homework: Record 1-minute strumming loop with 2 patterns.

Lesson 11: Hybrid Picking Basics
Objective: Combine pick and fingers for chicken-pickin’ and arpeggios.
Key Concepts:
- Pick: downstrokes on lower strings.
- Middle/ring fingers: pluck higher strings.

Practice Exercises:
1. Scale Exercise –
E|-----------------------------------|
B|-----------------------------------|
G|-------7-----9---------------------|
D|-----7-----9-----------------------|
A|---7-----9-------------------------|
E|-7---------------------------------|
   p m p m p (p = pick, m = middle)
2. Country Lick –
E|-----------------------------------|
B|-------12-15p12--------------------|
G|-14b16----------14-12--------------|
D|----------------------14-----------|
A|-----------------------------------|
E|-----------------------------------|
   (pick + middle finger snap on 15)
3. Arpeggio Pattern – Cmaj7 (pick bass, fingers treble).

Song Application: “Tennessee Whiskey” hybrid intro.

Homework: 1-minute hybrid picking phrase over G major.

Lesson 13: Bends, Vibrato & Expressive Techniques
Objective: Master pitch control and emotional phrasing.
Key Concepts:
- Bend types: half-step, whole-step, 1.5-step.
- Vibrato: wrist/forearm, narrow vs. wide.
- Pre-bends, release bends, unison bends.

Practice Exercises:
1. Whole-Step Bend Drill –
E|-------------------------|
B|-15b17---17r15-----------| (bend to G, hold, release)
   8x at 70 BPM.
2. Vibrato Ladder – Bend to pitch, add slow → fast vibrato.
3. Unison Bend Lick (Pentatonic Box 1) –
E|---------------------------------|
B|-15b17---17---15-----------------|
G|-------------------12---14b16----|

Song Application: “Hotel California” solo bends.

Homework: Record 30-sec solo focusing on expressive bends/vibrato.

- Sweep picking: continuous down/up motion across strings.
- Tapping: hammer with picking hand (e.g., 8-finger tap).
- Diminished & augmented arpeggios.

Practice Exercises:

Lesson 15: Transcribing & Ear Training
Objective: Develop the ability to learn songs by ear and transcribe solos.
Key Concepts:
- Interval recognition: perfect 4th/5th, major/minor 3rd.
- Phrase-by-phrase transcription method.
- Slow-down software tips (e.g., 50% speed, loop 2-bar sections).

Practice Exercises:
1. Interval ID – Play random intervals on guitar; name them.
2. Melody Transcription – Transcribe first 4 bars of “Happy Birthday” by ear.
3. Solo Phrase – Learn 8-bar lick from “Little Wing” intro (slowed).

Song Application: Transcribe main riff of “Black Dog” (Led Zeppelin).

Homework: Fully transcribe a 30-second guitar solo of choice; tab it out.
